Malaria

A mosquito-borne disease caused by Plasmodium parasites.


Symptoms:

  • High fever with chills
  • Sweating, especially after fever breaks
  • Headache and muscle pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Abdominal pain
  • Rapid breathing

Causes:

  • Bites from infected Anopheles mosquitoes
  • Transmission through blood transfusion, organ transplants, or sharing infected needles

Precautions:

  • Use mosquito nets and repellents
  • Wear long-sleeved clothing in mosquito-prone areas
  • Avoid stagnant water accumulation
  • Take antimalarial medications if traveling to high-risk areas

Medicines:

  • Antimalarials: Chloroquine, Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT), Quinine

  • Home Remedies:

    • Neem and papaya leaf extract (boosts immunity)
    • Ginger and cinnamon tea (reduces fever and chills)
    • Turmeric milk (has anti-inflammatory properties)

Food Recommendations:

  • Hydrating fluids (coconut water, fresh fruit juices)
  • High-protein diet (lentils, chicken, eggs)
  • Iron-rich foods (spinach, pomegranate)

Food to Avoid:

  • Sugary foods (cakes, candies, sodas) – cause blood sugar spikes
  • Refined carbs (white bread, white rice, pasta) – rapidly increase glucose levels
  • Fried foods – increase insulin resistance
  • Full-fat dairy – contains high saturated fat
  • Processed snacks – hidden sugars and unhealthy fats
